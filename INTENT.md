# INTENT

The one-page spine of Curiosity's Doors. Everything else answers to this.

> Living doc. Grows by chat. Last updated: 2026-05-17.

---

## What the game is

**A bunch of puzzles and obbys, interconnected.**

Each door in the hub opens into a realm. A realm is a puzzle, or an obby (obstacle course), or both — a thing you have to figure out *and* a thing you have to physically pull off. The realms link to each other; they aren't separate islands.

---

## The hero

Curiosity. **Genderless — they/them.** Cloaked. Hooded. Faceless. Carries a lantern. Cloak has eyes that blink.

**What they want:** answers to their own origin. They don't know where they came from. That question is what pulled them into the realm of doors in the first place — the doors are their way of looking.

**Curiosity speaks to the player through textboxes that appear on the player's screen.** They explain things — how they got here, why they're at the doors. They are not silent. This overrides earlier docs (`docs/STORY.md`, `docs/VIBE.md`) that called Curiosity wordless; those docs are kept as history but INTENT.md is the source of truth.

**Curiosity's voice is canonical** — Advika has already written it. The character of Curiosity appears in her novel **"Written by Silence"** (one of six personified emotions). Their voice is dry, sardonic, casually amused by cosmic stakes, fragmentary, observant. Full voice spec in `docs/VOICE.md`. The book is the reference; match it.

## The game is canon-set in the book's universe

**Decided 2026-05-17.** "Curiosity's Doors" takes place inside the world of *Written by Silence*. The book's mythology is the game's mythology:

- **Curiosity** is the hero (the same Curiosity from the book — genderless, cloak of blinking eyes, lantern, walks backwards through doors they shouldn't open).
- **Consciousness** is the architect of the world.
- **Silence** is the narrator / watcher who frames the story.
- **The Hollow** is a real place that may appear or be referenced.
- **The Tower** (and other tarot cards) carry the meaning the book gives them — they're cosmic objects, not decoration.
- **The other five emotions** (Fear, Grief, Joy, Anger, Love) exist in this universe and may be referenced or appear.

What this means for design: when in doubt about lore, the book has the answer. New realms can draw on the book's events, places, and tone. Curiosity's search for their origin is the same search the book sets up — they want to know what they are and where they came from.

What this *doesn't* mean: the game does not retell the book's plot. It is a parallel/extended journey within the same world. Players who haven't read the book should still understand the game on its own.

## The hub

**Not a quiet room — a topsy-turvy infinity of doors.** Stairs that lead nowhere. A clock that doesn't move. A sandglass that barely runs. Doors at every angle, every scale, everywhere you look. Question marks drift in the air. The hub is chaos held in deep purple, with the lantern as the only warm thing in it.

Full spec lives in `docs/HUB.md`. Visual reference dropped 2026-05-17.

## What "interconnected" means

The realms connect through **story**. Items collected carry lore weight.

- There is a storyline that follows Curiosity across the whole game.
- Each realm has its own significance to that storyline — it's not filler.
- Items collected in a realm are lore objects. In Realm 1 they also function mechanically (jade pieces → jade key → Door 2), but **that lock-and-key loop is a Realm 1 thing, not a universal pattern.**

## Every realm is its own thing

This is the rule that makes the game not a basic platformer:

- Each realm has its **own concept** — its own theme, its own world.
- Each realm has its **own way of playing** — different verbs, different challenges, different feel.
- The hub stays consistent. The realms diverge.

Realm 1 happens to be a platformer with a jade-key unlock. Realm 2 will play differently and unlock differently. We don't reuse Realm 1's loop — we invent.
