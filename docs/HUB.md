# The Hub

The room you keep coming back to. Where every door is. Not a level — a *place*.

> Living spec. Last updated: 2026-05-17. Visual reference dropped by Advika this session — see `assets/MANIFEST.md` for the reference image entry.

---

## The vibe

**Topsy-turvy. Chaotic. Disorganised.** Beautiful but wrong. Like a dream that forgot to pick a direction.

The hub is not a quiet stone chamber. It is a **place stuck in infinity** — you can feel that there's no edge to it, no real walls, no real ceiling. The architecture doesn't make sense and isn't trying to.

## What's in it
- **Doors everywhere.** Not three, not seven — *everywhere*. At different angles, different heights, different scales. Some are within reach. Some are floating. Some are upside-down.
- **Stairs that lead nowhere.** Escher-style. They climb to walls, to ceilings, to themselves.
- **A clock that's stagnant.** Hands frozen, or moving so slowly you can't catch them. **Prop — but it means something to the overall lore. (What, TBD.)**
- **A sandglass that's sort-of working.** Grains fall, but reluctantly. Time is broken here. **Prop — also lore-loaded. (What, TBD.)**
- **Question marks** drifting in the air, glowing on the floor — literal Curiosity. The hub is the player's question rendered as a room.

## Palette
Cool deep purples and violets. The lantern stays warm gold against it. (Matches existing art direction — purple base + gold lantern accent.)

## Feel rules
- The hub is **not safe**, but it's not hostile either — it's *unsettled*. Curiosity belongs here in the way a question belongs to a person who can't answer it.
- The hub doesn't progress in a straight line. Curiosity might exit one door and re-enter the hub from a different angle.
- Doors that have been completed look different — but Advika hasn't decided how yet. *(TBD)*
- When all doors are locked (the Realm 1 → Door 2 forging moment), the chaos intensifies — locks visibly close, the clock and sandglass stutter, the room holds its breath until the jade key is made.

## Text in the hub

The hub itself has **no signs, no labels, no rules-as-words baked into the art**. The phrases on the reference image are art-flavor, not design.

But — **Curiosity speaks to the player through textboxes** in the hub (and elsewhere). They explain how they got here, why they're at the doors. This is intentional and important. See "Curiosity speaks" in `INTENT.md`. So: no environmental text, *yes* Curiosity-voice textboxes.

## Open questions

- The clock and sandglass — are they just decor, or do they *do* something? (Slow down when Curiosity stops moving? Tick when a realm is solved?)
- Doors that have been completed look different — how?
